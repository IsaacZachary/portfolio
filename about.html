    From Technical Support to Infrastructure Development

    Background & Foundation

    My career began in technical support and troubleshooting—resolving network issues, configuring systems, and helping users navigate technical challenges. This foundation gave me deep empathy for end-users and a practical understanding of what breaks in production systems.

    Working with ISPs and small businesses, I saw recurring patterns: manual deployments that failed, inconsistent environments, and systems that were hard to maintain. I realized I wanted to build systems that prevent these issues, not just fix the symptoms.

    Transition to Development & DevOps

    I started building applications to solve real problems—first simple scripts, then full-stack applications, and eventually containerized systems. Each project taught me something new: how to design APIs, manage databases, secure deployments, and automate repetitive tasks.
